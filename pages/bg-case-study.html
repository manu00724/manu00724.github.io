01

Context

Relationship Managers and Operations teams manually validated Customer Requirement Lists (CRLs) against generated Bank Guarantees before issuance. The workflow involved extracting entities from scanned CRLs, verifying customer information in the BG, and reviewing legal clauses for compliance. This manual process was time-consuming, inconsistent, and difficult to scale.

02

Problem & cost

Quantify the pain before proposing a fix — this is what separates a PM case study from an engineering one.

The existing workflow relied on manual document review and comparison across multiple documents. Analysts manually extracted entities, validated customer details, and identified onerous clauses before every Bank Guarantee was issued.

  • Volume: Hundreds of Bank Guarantees processed monthly
  • Time cost: Average processing time of 55 minutes per Bank Guarantee
  • Quality / risk cost: Risk of missed entity mismatches and non-standard contractual clauses
  • Why "just add more people" didn't scale: Document formats varied significantly and required domain expertise

My role & process

I led product discovery, solution design, prioritization, and delivery with AI, backend, and frontend engineers while working closely with business stakeholders.

  • Discovery: Mapped the BG issuance workflow and identified manual review bottlenecks
  • Definition: Defined entity schemas, clause categories, PRD, and MVP scope
  • Build: Selected LLMs, designed validation workflow, and reviewed extraction quality
  • Launch: Conducted UAT and iterative improvements before deployment
05

Key decisions & trade-offs

The two or three calls that actually shaped the product — and what was given up each time.

Should one model handle all document types?

Considered

Single multimodal LLM for both CRLs and BGs

Chosen — and why

Used Llama 3.2 Vision for CRL images and Gemma 3.0 for BG text because each model performed better on its input modality.

How should extracted entities be compared?

Considered

Exact string matching

Chosen — and why

Used Levenshtein distance to tolerate OCR noise and formatting differences in long address fields.

06

Architecture & stack

CRL images are processed by Llama 3.2 Vision to extract structured entities. Generated Bank Guarantees are processed by Gemma 3.0 to extract entities and classify onerous clauses. Extracted entities are compared using Levenshtein distance and the results are presented in a review interface for human validation before approval.

08

Results & impact

Business impact

  • Reduced Bank Guarantee processing time by ~30 minutes.
  • Automated extraction and validation across CRLs and BGs.
  • Standardized onerous clause identification and review.
09

Challenges & what I'd do differently

What was harder than expected
  • Highly variable CRL layouts and scanned document quality.
  • Building confidence in AI-assisted validation for regulated workflows.
What I'd change next time
  • Establish automated evaluation pipelines earlier.
  • Introduce confidence-based routing for high-confidence approvals.
10

Key learnings

  • Choosing specialized models for each modality often outperforms a single general-purpose model.
  • Enterprise AI products require explainability, evaluation, and human oversight to build trust.
